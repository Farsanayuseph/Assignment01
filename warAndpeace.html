War and Peace

War and Peace is a well-known 19th-century Russian novel, written and published by author Leo Tolstoy in 1867. It shows the epic story of Russian society as it was during the 1812 French invasion of Russia. The story follows three famous characters in world literature: Natasha Rostov, Prince Andrei Bolkonsky, and Pierre Bezukhov. Tolstoy follows each character, all of whom come from different backgrounds; from peasants to nobility, from civilians to soldiers. The reader watches them struggle with issues distinct to the 1800s and watches them grow from the start to finish in all ways: personally, emotionally, historically, and culturally.

Though this was Tolstoy's first great war novel, he went in to publish other famous works such as Anna Karenina (1877) and The Death of Ivan Ilyich (1886). Tolstoy enjoyed writing novels about the mundane aspects of everyday life, and the relationships between humans. War and Peace won critical acclamation due to its vivid exploration of Russian philosophy and history. Critics credit the novel's success to Tolstoy's capacity to invent realistic fictional characters who suffer from the true human experience.
